Śatānanda – Ancient Indian Scholar

12th Century Mathematician • Commentator • Astronomer

About Śatānanda

Śatānanda was a prominent 12th-century Indian mathematician and scholar. He is well-known for his detailed commentary on the Bṛhatsaṁhitā, a major astronomical and scientific text of ancient India. His interpretations helped preserve and expand classical Indian mathematical and astronomical knowledge.

Major Contributions

  • Commentary on the ancient astronomical text Bṛhatsaṁhitā.
  • Analytical explanations of planetary motions.
  • Preservation of earlier astronomical traditions.
  • Interpretations of natural and celestial events.

Legacy

Śatānanda’s works remain valuable resources for historians and researchers studying Indian astronomy. His writings form an important link between classical ancient texts and medieval scholarly development.
